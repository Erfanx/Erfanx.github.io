Black Mirror - Metalhead

Black Mirror's Metalhead wants to warn us about the technological disordering in the near future

Experience the horrific robot dog of your worst nightmares

By Syed Irfan | March 27, 2020
Black Mirror is an anthology Netflix series of Charlie Brooker. The fourth season of this series was released on Netflix on December 29th, 2017.

There are three of them. They are heading towards a warehouse in a car. There motive is to break into the warehouse to steal something for a dying family member of Bella’s. They are searching for there desired thing very consciously as any of the technological security systems can't catch them. But after they enter the facility, the robotic dog appears, shoots tracking projectiles into Bella’s crew, and kills one of the crews of her. It even takes control of a vehicle to force Bella off the road, setting up a desperate run as she tries to save herself. At that time it kills another crew of her and she becomes alone.

There’s no time to realize whether the robotic dog has a natural brain or computer-generated when watching the episode. Slade the director of Metalhead shoots the robot simply, as if it’s utterly grounded in reality, and whether it’s chasing an escaping van or tracking Bella through the rocks alongside a river, the approach paints the focus on the creature as an unstoppable, cringe-inducing moments in the entire series.

Beyond the thrills of the chase itself, there is no hope for a happy ending in “Metalhead.” There is only the final ending.

One of the scariest things about Metalhead‘s robots is their lack of artificial intelligence: their lack of empathy or emotional nuance means they’re entirely ruled by cold, pre-programmed logic. If they sense an intruder, then their job is to neutralise the threat – it doesn’t matter whether the intruder wanted to steal equipment worth millions or a stuffed toy worth a few pence. -Ryan Lambie, DEN OF GEEK.
