Can you live forever uploading your mind?

Can you live forever uploading your mind?

Exactly what we have to upload?

By Syed Irfan | December 11, 2020
Exactly what we have to upload? The mind is something that is hard to find. It is said to be the collective abilities of consciousness and intelligence, the thing that let you imagine, recognize, think, and dream. The concept of mind uploading is a hypothetical concept of uploading a copy of the mind and transfer it into a computer to run a simulation of consciousness.

The possibility of mind uploading is based on three assumptions. They are -- Physicalism, Scanability, and Computability. Your mind exists in your brain structure, arrangement, and biochemistry. The idea of the mind can be found in the physical brain is called physicalism. Scanability is the digital copy of a physical brain that can be simulated possessing the technology. Computer software can host your mind, which means your mind is computable, which is called the Computability. There is no physical property of the brain including consciousness that can’t be simulated accurately even if it requires a lot of code.



To think about this first we have to know what is the brain. The brain is the most complex biological structure. Around 100 billion neurons are communicating through 1 million billion connections, that send up to 1000 signals per second. The most developed part of the brain is the Neocortex. It holds memorization, the ability to plan, think, imagine, hope, and dream. In which part the “YOU” existed is not clear yet. The part precuneus has the greatest influence on our consciousness. The building blocks of the brain are not simple either. We have a basic understanding of how the neuron works and we can accurately predict their behavior. But there are a lot more than just neurons and the nervous system in our brain. The hormone plays an important role, which affects our mood, helps us to learn, etc. The brain is also affected by heart nerves. It will get more complicated as we get to know more about it.

To get these widely interconnected massive cells and others into a computer, we need a model that can simulate it in our digital world. Some sort of scan can be helpful. But the scanning technology is not that developed yet to attempt such a thing. But there is a method that seems very promising. Cutting a brain into tiny slices and scan them.
